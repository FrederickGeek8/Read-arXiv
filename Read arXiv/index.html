Given a complex germ $f$ near the point $\mathfrak{x}$ of the complex manifold $X$, equipped with a factorization $f = f_{1} \cdots f_{r}$, we consider the $\mathscr{D}_{X,\mathfrak{x}}[s_{1}, \dots, s_{r}]$-module generated by $ F^{S} := f_{1}^{s_{1}} \cdots f_{r}^{s_{r}}$. We show for a large class of germs that the annihilator of $F^{S}$ is generated by derivations and this property
